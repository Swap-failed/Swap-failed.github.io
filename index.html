What is Swap Failed?

Uniswap is the official app destination for retrying an EVM token trade after a wallet rejection, stale quote, tight slippage limit, or network issue. The protocol is a non-custodial automated market maker documented in the Uniswap trading docs.

How Swap Failed works

  1. Open the appUse the official Uniswap swap page and confirm the domain before connecting a wallet.
  2. Check the pairReview the input token, output token, network, and token contract before signing.
  3. Refresh executionGet a fresh quote, review price impact, slippage tolerance, and available gas on the selected chain.
  4. Retry carefullySubmit only after the wallet simulation and final transaction details match what you expect.

Before you swap

Official route

The action belongs in the Uniswap app, not on a copied error page or lookalike domain.

Execution checks

Most rejected trades come down to wallet rejection, expired quotes, insufficient gas, tight slippage, unsupported tokens, or contract restrictions.

Onchain context

Uniswap contract deployments span Ethereum and several EVM networks, so the selected chain must match the asset you intend to trade.

Swap Failed FAQ

Why did my swap failed transaction happen?

A trade can fail when the quote expires, the token moves outside your slippage limit, gas is insufficient, the wallet rejects signing, or the token contract blocks the route.

What should I check after swap failed in my wallet?

Check the official domain, selected network, token contract, wallet balance for gas, price impact, and any warning shown before signing again.

Can swap failed still cost gas?

Yes. If a transaction reaches the chain and reverts, network validators can still charge gas for the attempted execution.

Is swap failed a Uniswap problem every time?

No. The failure may come from wallet settings, chain congestion, token behavior, approval state, stale routing, or your chosen execution limits.

Where should I go when swap failed?

Use the official Uniswap app at app.uniswap.org/swap and avoid signing transactions from unofficial clones or support links.
