Why swaps fail

Fix the route before you retry

A failed swap can come from slippage, expired quotes, insufficient gas, unsupported assets, bridge congestion, or a wallet rejection. The right next move is to inspect the route instead of rushing into another signature.

Retry with a fresh quote

Quotes can expire quickly when liquidity or gas changes. Open the Swap Failed app, rebuild the route, and review the live receive token before you confirm.

Keep custody in your wallet

The flow is non-custodial: you connect a wallet such as MetaMask, inspect the transaction, and sign only when the route matches what you expect.

Check chains and assets

Swaps involving Ethereum, BTC routes, stablecoins, and wrapped assets may fail when the source chain, destination chain, or token contract is not supported for that route.

How it works

Resolve Swap Failed in three steps

1

Reconnect and inspect

Open the app, connect your wallet, and select the asset pair that failed. The interface rebuilds the route so you can see the current quote and network path.

2

Review time and cost

Check the live quote, chain fee prompts, estimated completion window, and destination asset before signing. Costs depend on the networks and liquidity available at confirmation.

3

Confirm only when ready

Approve or sign from your wallet after the route looks correct. If a wallet prompt looks unfamiliar, stop and verify the token, chain, and receiving address first.

FAQ

Swap Failed questions

01

What does Swap Failed mean?

Swap Failed means the requested token exchange did not complete on the selected route. It can happen because a quote expired, gas was insufficient, liquidity changed, the wallet rejected the signature, or the route could not finish across the chosen chains.

02

Are my funds safe after a failed swap?

Often a failed transaction leaves funds in your wallet or source chain, but you should verify the transaction hash, token balance, and wallet activity. Use explorers such as Etherscan for Ethereum activity and avoid signing a new approval until you understand the previous result.

03

Which chains and assets can I retry?

The app is built for major crypto swap routes including ETH, BTC-linked routes, stablecoins, and popular EVM assets. Availability depends on live liquidity and route support; see the full list on the supported chains page.

04

How long does a failed swap take to clear?

Timing depends on the chain, gas market, route type, and bridge or liquidity provider involved. The app shows the current route status in app, while broader DeFi context can be checked through DeFiLlama.

05

What does it cost to retry?

There is no fixed cost shown on this preview page. Review the live network fee, quote, and any token approval prompt inside the app before you sign, because costs change with network demand and route conditions.

06

Should I revoke old approvals?

If the failed swap involved a token approval you no longer need, review it with a trusted allowance tool before revoking or re-approving. Revocation also requires a wallet transaction, so check the chain and contract carefully.
